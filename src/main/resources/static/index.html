01
Direction 1 — SWS to Departments
When a business updates its registered address in SWS, OmniSync receives the canonical event. It uses UBID to identify matching records in every department system, translates the payload using per-department schema adapters (SOAP/XML for Shops, SQL for Factories, REST for GST), and confirms each write with a correlation ID. Retries are idempotent — the same change arriving twice will never produce a duplicate write.
02
Direction 2 — Departments to SWS
When a department system updates a record directly (e.g. Factories changes the authorised signatory), OmniSync discovers the change through the best available surface — a webhook if the system emits one, or a scheduled sidecar poller that compares snapshots for silent systems. The change is translated into the SWS canonical format, joined on UBID, and written back to SWS with a full audit entry.
03
Conflict Detection & Resolution
When two updates for the same UBID arrive from different sources within a short window (configurable, default 5 seconds), OmniSync detects the conflict and applies a clear resolution policy: SWS wins on canonical identity fields (registered address), while department-owned operational fields (authorised signatory) can be merged. Every decision is written to the audit ledger with the reason, so the final state is always explainable and reversible.
🏛 Karnataka Sample Scenario
Step 1 — SWS address update: Business KA-BIZ-00192 updates its registered address to 12 MG Road, Bengaluru in SWS. OmniSync picks up the event, looks up the UBID in its adapter registry, and propagates the change to Shops & Establishments (as SOAP/XML) and Factories (as SQL UPDATE). Both writes are confirmed and logged.

Step 2 — Dept signatory update: The Factories system directly updates the authorised signatory to Rajesh Kumar. OmniSync's sidecar poller detects the delta on the next polling cycle, translates the Factories-specific payload back to SWS canonical format, and writes the update to SWS — identified by UBID.

Step 3 — Conflict: Two updates for KA-BIZ-00192 arrive nearly simultaneously — SWS changes address, Factories changes signatory. OmniSync detects the conflict window, applies the merge policy (SWS wins address; Factories wins signatory), writes both to the canonical record, and records the resolution decision in the audit trail.
